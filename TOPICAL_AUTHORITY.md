# Topical Authority — epoxy.co.id

## Role and boundary

Epoxy.co.id should become an Indonesian reference for epoxy as a material family and engineered system: chemistry, formulation, specification, substrate assessment, application, performance, failure diagnosis, repair, maintenance, safety, procurement, and evidence-led commercial support. Its primary readers are asset owners, facility and maintenance teams, designers, specifiers, buyers, contractors, and technically curious consumers in Indonesia.

The domain may cover epoxy flooring in depth because flooring is a major epoxy application. Within this domain, neutral education must remain distinct from `/epoxy-lantai`, `/cat-epoxy`, and other commercial routes. General flooring selection belongs to Lantai.id; contractor qualification and project delivery may also be covered independently by KontraktorEpoxy.co.id; EpoxyLantai.co.id can independently publish a flooring-specialist viewpoint. Those are separate domains, so their overlap is an editorial opportunity, not cannibalization.

Existing non-epoxy services—general concrete casting, floor hardener, waterproofing, and non-epoxy injection—should not determine the future knowledge boundary. Retain commercially useful routes while reviewing whether each has a defensible relationship to epoxy systems.

## Existing coverage and risks

- The site is commercially clear but editorially thin relative to its URL count. Location templates and archives must not be counted as topical authority.
- Hundreds of location-swapped pages create doorway, quality, crawl-allocation, and same-domain overlap risk. Several duplicate suffixes (`-2`) and malformed regional names need inventory-level review.
- The video sitemap preserves `.html` and slash variants that can compete with canonical non-extension routes unless redirects and canonicals are verified live.
- Existing articles repeatedly target “benefits,” “applications,” “choosing epoxy,” and “how to apply” without sufficiently different reader jobs. The consolidation plan assigns one owner per intent.
- Commercial pages mix material education, service sales, prices, and application guidance. Planned neutral references must link to them contextually without duplicating their transactional intent.
- Health, chemical resistance, food/healthcare suitability, anti-static performance, slip resistance, emissions, fire behavior, and waterproofing claims are high-stakes. They require product-specific data, current standards, and competent review.
- The repository contains broad concrete, waterproofing, and floor-hardener service clusters. Expanding those as general encyclopedias here would dilute the epoxy entity; only epoxy relationships and comparison decisions belong in the editorial program.

## Internal-link architecture

- `/` links to the EPX-01 fundamentals hub, EPX-06 selection hub, EPX-12 defect hub, EPX-15 estimating hub, and verified commercial services.
- Every article links upward to its topic hub (`TOPIC:EPX-xx`); each hub lists all eight children.
- EPX-01–EPX-03 form the knowledge foundation. EPX-04 → EPX-05 → EPX-06 → EPX-07 → EPX-16 forms the project-delivery sequence.
- EPX-08–EPX-10 use-case pages link back to EPX-06 selection and forward to EPX-13 maintenance. They link to commercial routes only when the reader has a relevant project need.
- EPX-12 diagnostic pages link to the relevant EPX-04/05/07 prevention page, EPX-11 repair choice, and EPX-13 replace/recoat decision.
- EPX-14 safety pages are mandatory contextual links from mixing, surface preparation, occupied-building, storage, spill, and waste content.
- Calculators and procurement tools in EPX-15 link to the system-selection assumptions in EPX-06 and scope/QC documents in EPX-16.
- No fixed “related posts” block should be copied across the catalog; the row-level relationships in `ARTICLE_CATALOG.md` define intent-specific links.

## Evidence and editorial standards

- Use current manufacturer TDS and SDS for product-specific mix, coverage, cure, exposure, storage, and safety claims; never generalize one product sheet to all epoxies.
- Verify named standards at publication time through the issuing organization. Summarize test purpose and limitations; do not invent thresholds or imply certification from an article.
- Chemical resistance, food/healthcare suitability, potable-water contact, emissions, fire behavior, conductivity, slip, and structural repair claims require qualified expert review and application-specific evidence.
- Prices, service life, warranties, and cure/downtime values must be dated, sourced, and stated as conditional ranges or product/project facts—not universal promises.
- Case studies require real project authorization, original images, documented conditions, system, dates, controls, and results. Never fabricate experience.
- High-risk procedures require prerequisites, PPE/engineering controls, stop conditions, and a clear point for specialist intervention.
- Location content is publishable only when local conditions, regulations, supply, documented service operation, or a real case materially change the page.
- Maintain a source log, review date, named reviewer role, and update trigger for every P0/P1 article.
