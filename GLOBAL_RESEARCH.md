## Purpose

This file is the shared pre-writing evidence layer for the complete `epoxy.co.id` catalog. It consolidates recurring chemistry, substrate, preparation, testing, execution, safety, maintenance, procurement, and handover research once, then maps it to every parent topic.

It is not a product selection, specification, safety data sheet, application method, structural repair design, legal opinion, price list, warranty, or permission to claim that an installed floor or coating meets a test result. Writers must open and cite the original source and resolve the stated gates.

## How writers must use this file

1. Start with the topic coverage row, then open every mapped original source.
2. Keep uncured resin/hardener hazards separate from a fully cured, verified system.
3. Keep resin chemistry, formulated product, multi-layer system, substrate, workmanship, and installed performance separate.
4. Treat a public standards abstract as proof of identity and visible scope only; exact procedures and acceptance values require the current full standard.
5. Never convert one laboratory result into a universal installed-floor claim.
6. Use the exact current TDS, SDS, batch, substrate, environment, and project specification before numeric or procedural advice.
7. Preserve stop conditions for moisture, contamination, weak substrate, movement, unsafe exposure, structural work, and occupied operations.
8. Recheck volatile sources during outlining or six months after the date above, whichever comes first.

### KR-02 — Epoxy resin vocabulary and test-method families

- **Sources:** [ISO 18280:2010 epoxy-resin test-method overview](https://www.iso.org/standard/52877.html), [ISO 7142:2023 binders for paints and varnishes](https://www.iso.org/standard/83331.html), [ISO 3673-2:2012 crosslinked epoxy specimens/properties](https://www.iso.org/standard/59248.html).
- **Grade:** B.
- **Purpose:** Ground chemistry and testing articles without treating “epoxy” as one uniform product.
- **Summary:** ISO maintains different methods for uncrosslinked epoxy resins, epoxy binders used in paints/varnishes, and properties of prepared crosslinked specimens.
- **Grounded facts:** Resin, curing agent, modifiers, pigments, fillers, aggregate, solvent/water, film build, substrate, cure, and specimen preparation can all change measured behavior.
- **Incorporation:** Use in `EPX-01`, `EPX-02`, `EPX-03`, `EPX-06`. Define the test object and system boundary before comparing a property.
- **Limits/recheck:** These abstracts do not provide formulations, mixing ratios, acceptance values, or installed-floor requirements.

### KR-03 — Cure, crosslinking, and the limit of “dry”

- **Sources:** [ISO 14322:2018 DSC crosslinking method](https://www.iso.org/standard/74606.html), [ISO 20368:2017 FTIR crosslinking method](https://www.iso.org/standard/67794.html).
- **Grade:** B.
- **Purpose:** Prevent articles from equating surface dryness with complete cure or universal readiness for service.
- **Summary:** ISO methods determine degree of crosslinking using defined laboratory techniques; one method explicitly has applicability limits for fast-reacting systems.
- **Grounded facts:** Pot life, working time, tack-free time, recoat window, light foot traffic, full cure, and chemical-service readiness are different milestones defined by the actual product and conditions.
- **Incorporation:** Use in `EPX-01`, `EPX-02`, `EPX-03`, `EPX-07`, `EPX-12`, `EPX-16`. Require the exact TDS, recorded temperature/humidity, batch times, and cure criterion.
- **Limits/recheck:** Do not infer site cure from an abstract, touch test, odor, calendar time, or another product's data.

### KR-05 — Concrete moisture testing is a bounded measurement

- **Sources:** [ASTM F2170 in-situ relative-humidity method](https://store.astm.org/standards/f2170), [ASTM flooring-committee reference list](https://mcsdocs.astm.org/committee-documents/F06_Fact_Sheet_2017.pdf).
- **Grade:** B.
- **Purpose:** Ground substrate-moisture diagnosis and stop “concrete looks dry” reasoning.
- **Summary:** ASTM F2170 measures relative humidity at defined locations/depths and states that results describe the slab condition only at the test time and locations.
- **Grounded facts:** Moisture source/path, slab construction, vapor retarder, drying conditions, test placement/equilibration, ambient conditions, and product limits matter. One reading cannot map an entire slab or guarantee future behavior.
- **Incorporation:** Use in `EPX-04`, `EPX-06`, `EPX-07`, `EPX-12`, `EPX-16`. Document slab history, method/edition, probe locations, calibration, conditions, result map, and product acceptance rule.
- **Limits/recheck:** Obtain the current full method and exact manufacturer/project criteria. Do not mix methods or publish a universal threshold.

### KR-06 — Concrete surface preparation and profile

- **Sources:** [ICRI 310.2R surface-preparation guideline record](https://store.icri.org/item/3102r2013-english-pdf-selecting-concrete-surface-preparation-sealers-coatings-polymer-overlays-concrete-repair-342521), [ICRI introduction to 310.2R](https://www.icri.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/CRBMayJun14_WInkler.pdf).
- **Grade:** C for visible scope; Gate for full requirements.
- **Purpose:** Ground preparation selection in substrate, coating, site, profile, and QC rather than one favorite machine.
- **Summary:** ICRI's process evaluates substrate condition, the material to be installed, and job-site conditions before selecting preparation methods and verification criteria.
- **Grounded facts:** Cleaning, contaminant removal, laitance/weak-layer removal, soundness, profile, edges, cracks/joints, dust control, and verification are separate tasks.
- **Incorporation:** Use in `EPX-04`, `EPX-05`, `EPX-06`, `EPX-07`, `EPX-12`, `EPX-16`. Build survey/preparation hold points and match profile to current system instructions.
- **Limits/recheck:** Do not reproduce paid tables or assign a CSP number without the governing guide, product requirements, substrate test, and competent review.

### KR-07 — Grinding concrete can create respirable silica

- **Sources:** [OSHA floor-grinder silica control fact sheet](https://www.osha.gov/sites/default/files/publications/OSHA3932.pdf), [OSHA crystalline-silica overview](https://www.osha.gov/silica-crystalline).
- **Grade:** C; US regulation/guidance, not Indonesian law.
- **Purpose:** Prevent surface-preparation articles from presenting dry grinding, dust cleanup, or PPE as casual DIY detail.
- **Summary:** OSHA identifies concrete grinding as a respirable-crystalline-silica source and describes wet methods or suitable vacuum dust collection as engineering-control approaches.
- **Grounded facts:** Dust generation depends on material, task, equipment, enclosure, duration, controls, maintenance, and cleanup. Dried slurry/dust can create secondary exposure.
- **Incorporation:** Use in `EPX-05`, `EPX-07`, `EPX-08`, `EPX-09`, `EPX-14`, `EPX-16`. Require an Indonesian K3 assessment, engineering controls, restricted area, cleanup plan, and competent respiratory review.
- **Limits/recheck:** Do not import US exposure limits or equipment prescriptions as Indonesian compliance.

### KR-08 — Adhesion testing and failure mode

- **Sources:** [ASTM D7234 pull-off adhesion on concrete](https://store.astm.org/d7234-21.html), [ASTM coating standards index](https://store.astm.org/products-services/standards-and-publications/standards/paint-standards-and-related-coating-standards.html).
- **Grade:** B.
- **Purpose:** Ground adhesion specifications and explain why a single number without failure mode/test setup is incomplete.
- **Summary:** D7234 evaluates perpendicular pull-off adhesion of coatings on concrete and requires reporting the system's weakest failure plane; instrument, fixture, substrate, cure, and method affect results.
- **Grounded facts:** Failure may occur in glue, coating layers, interface, or substrate. The test is destructive and leaves repair locations.
- **Incorporation:** Use in `EPX-03`, `EPX-04`, `EPX-05`, `EPX-12`, `EPX-16`. Predefine method/edition, instrument, locations, cure, result/failure-mode reporting, acceptance, and repair.
- **Limits/recheck:** Obtain the full current method and project specification. Do not compare unlike instruments, substrates, systems, or cure states as equivalent.

### KR-09 — Abrasion and chemical tests are method-specific

- **Sources:** [ASTM D4060 abrasion method](https://store.astm.org/d4060-19.html), [ASTM D1308 household-chemical effects](https://store.astm.org/standards/d1308), [ASTM D01 current standards overview](https://www.astm.org/v3/assets/blt5eb0a2cb04534832/blt00cec9a555817198/69d75d173445405e438dc789/D01_Fact_Sheet_2025.pdf).
- **Grade:** B.
- **Purpose:** Stop “abrasion resistant” and “chemical resistant” from becoming unbounded marketing claims.
- **Summary:** D4060 evaluates abrasion under defined wheels/specimens and warns about variability; D1308 evaluates observable effects of defined household chemicals on continuous coating films.
- **Grounded facts:** Abradant, cycles, load, specimen, thickness, cure, cleaning, exposure chemical/concentration/time/temperature, and evaluation criterion change the meaning of a result.
- **Incorporation:** Use in `EPX-03`, `EPX-06`, `EPX-08`, `EPX-09`, `EPX-10`, `EPX-13`, `EPX-16`. State method and exact exposure; request the original report tied to the offered system.
- **Limits/recheck:** A laboratory result is not service-life, slip, hygiene, impact, or whole-floor proof.

### KR-10 — Slip resistance requires a defined surface and method

- **Sources:** [ASTM pedestrian/walkway safety standards collection](https://www.astm.org/products-services/standards-and-publications/standards/pedestrian-walkway-safety-standards.html), [ASTM D01 coatings committee overview](https://www.astm.org/v3/assets/blt5eb0a2cb04534832/blt00cec9a555817198/69d75d173445405e438dc789/D01_Fact_Sheet_2025.pdf).

- **Purpose:** Prevent generic “anti-slip” claims and invented coefficients.
- **Summary:** Slip evaluation belongs to defined pedestrian/walkway methods and cannot be inferred from resin type or aggregate presence alone.
- **Grounded facts:** Surface texture, wear, contamination, wet/dry condition, footwear, slope, cleaning, test device, protocol, and user population can affect risk and measured values.
- **Incorporation:** Use in `EPX-03`, `EPX-06`, `EPX-08`, `EPX-09`, `EPX-13`, `EPX-16`. Define scenario, governing method, test condition, sampling, acceptance, and maintenance.
- **Limits/recheck:** Obtain the exact current applicable Indonesian/project method and qualified safety/accessibility review before a number or compliance claim.

### KR-11 — System selection starts with actual exposure

- **Sources:** [ISO 12944-2:2017 environment classification](https://www.iso.org/standard/64834.html), [ISO 12944-5:2019 protective paint systems](https://www.iso.org/standard/77795.html), [ISO 12944-6:2018 laboratory performance methods](https://www.iso.org/standard/51378.html).
- **Grade:** B; steel-corrosion context, transferable only as decision logic.
- **Purpose:** Provide a defensible exposure-to-system decision structure for floors and protective coatings.
- **Summary:** ISO 12944 separates environment classification, system selection, and laboratory testing, and explicitly says laboratory results aid selection rather than predict exact durability.
- **Grounded facts:** Substrate, chemicals, water/immersion, UV, temperature/cycling, traffic/impact/abrasion, hygiene/cleaning, downtime, joints/movement, and maintenance access are separate inputs.
- **Incorporation:** Use in `EPX-06`, `EPX-08`, `EPX-09`, `EPX-10`, `EPX-13`, `EPX-16`. Create a requirement matrix and compare complete documented systems, not generic resin names.
- **Limits/recheck:** ISO 12944 is primarily steel corrosion and several parts are marked for revision. Do not use its categories as floor requirements without applicable project adoption.

### KR-14 — Epoxy bonding/repair classifications and exclusions

- **Sources:** [ASTM C881/C881M epoxy bonding systems for concrete](https://store.astm.org/c0881_c0881m-20a.html), [ISO 16311-3:2024 repair design](https://www.iso.org/standard/79787.html), [ISO 16311-4:2024 repair execution](https://www.iso.org/standard/79788.html).

### KR-15 — Epoxy exposure, sensitization, and uncured components

- **Sources:** [CDC/NIOSH epoxy and reproductive-health guidance](https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/reproductive-health/prevention/epoxies-resins.html), [CDC/NIOSH skin exposure overview](https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/skin-exposure/about/), [NIOSH epoxy hazard alert record](https://stacks.cdc.gov/view/cdc/135632).
- **Grade:** C; authoritative occupational-health guidance, not Indonesian regulation.
- **Purpose:** Ground product-specific safety content and reject odor-based safety judgments.
- **Summary:** NIOSH notes that uncured resin-system components can be hazardous, epoxy/acrylic resins can cause skin disease/sensitization, and cured materials are generally safer to handle than uncured components.
- **Grounded facts:** Resin, hardener, solvents, fillers, pigments, cleaners, and reaction by-products may have different hazards. Smell is not a reliable exposure indicator; glove compatibility and ventilation are product/task specific.
- **Incorporation:** Use in `EPX-01`, `EPX-02`, `EPX-07`, `EPX-12`, `EPX-14`, `EPX-15`, `EPX-16`. Require current SDS/labels, substitution/control hierarchy, hygiene, spill/emergency plan, and occupational review.
- **Limits/recheck:** Do not diagnose exposure, prescribe PPE generically, or present US requirements as Indonesian law.
